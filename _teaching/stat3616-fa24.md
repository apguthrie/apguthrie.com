Undergraduate introductory statistics course covering analysis of variance, categorical data analysis, non-parametric tests, simple linear regression, multiple regression, and logistic regression with applications in biological sciences. Biweekly 75-minute lectures (71 students). **Taught in Fall 2024.**

**Course Objectives:**
Students will learn to apply basic concepts of statistical analysis with a focus on statistical thinking and interpretation. Students will be able to perform the analyses using R.

**Textbooks:**

*Introductory Statistics for the Life and Biomedical Sciences*, 1st Edition, by Julie Vu and David Harrington.

*The Practice of Statistics in the Life Sciences*, (any edition), by Brigitte Baldi and David Moore. ISBN-13: 978-1-4641-7536-7 and ISBN-10: 1-4641-7536-5
